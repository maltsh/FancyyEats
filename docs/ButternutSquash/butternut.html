Roasted Butternut Squash Recipe:

Ingredients:

  • 1 butternut squash, about 2-3 pounds
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1-2 cloves garlic, minced (optional)
  • 1-2 teaspoons fresh thyme leaves (or 1/2 teaspoon dried thyme)
  • Salt and pepper, to taste

Instructions:

  • Preheat the Oven:
  • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
  • Prepare the Butternut Squash:
  • Wash the butternut squash and peel it with a vegetable peeler. Cut the squash in half lengthwise and scoop out the seeds and stringy pulp. Cut the peeled squash into bite-sized cubes, roughly 1-inch in size.
  • Season the Squash:
  • In a large bowl, toss the butternut squash cubes with olive oil, minced garlic (if using), fresh thyme leaves (or dried thyme), salt, and pepper. Ensure the squash is evenly coated with the seasonings.
  • Roast the Squash:
  • Spread the seasoned squash cubes in a single layer on a baking sheet lined with parchment paper or a silicone baking mat. Make sure they're not crowded, so they roast evenly. You can use two baking sheets if needed.
  • Bake:
  • Roast the butternut squash in the preheated oven for about 25-30 minutes, or until the squash is tender and caramelized, with slightly crispy edges. You can give them a gentle stir or flip them halfway through the cooking time to ensure even browning.
  • Serve:
  • Once roasted to your liking, remove the butternut squash from the oven and let it cool for a few minutes. Serve it as a delicious side dish, add it to salads, pasta, or use it as a base for soups or other dishes.

This roasted butternut squash is a great addition to your fall and winter meals.

It's both flavorful and versatile, making it a wonderful choice for various culinary creations.
