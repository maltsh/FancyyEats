HARD BOILED EGGS
RECENT POST

Ingredients:

Eggs (as many as you want to hard-boil)

Instructions:

Choose Fresh Eggs:

For the best results, use fresh eggs. The fresher the eggs, the easier they are to peel.

Prepare a Saucepan:

Place the eggs in a single layer in a saucepan. You can hard-boil as many eggs as you like, but make sure they are in a single layer at the bottom of the pan.

Add Water:

Add enough water to the saucepan to cover the eggs with about an inch of water.

Heat the Water:

Place the saucepan on the stovetop over medium-high heat and bring the water to a boil.

Reduce to a Simmer:

Once the water reaches a rolling boil, reduce the heat to low, cover the saucepan with a lid, and let the eggs simmer for 9-12 minutes, depending on your desired yolk consistency.

For soft-boiled eggs with a runny yolk, simmer for 4-6 minutes.

For hard-boiled eggs with a firm yolk, simmer for 9-12 minutes.

Prepare an Ice Bath:

While the eggs are simmering, prepare a large bowl of ice water. You'll use this to cool the eggs rapidly after cooking.

Cool the Eggs:

After the eggs have finished cooking, immediately transfer them to the ice water bath. Let them sit in the ice water for at least 5 minutes. This not only stops the cooking process but also makes the eggs easier to peel.

Peel the Eggs:

Gently tap each egg on a hard surface to crack the shell, then roll it between your hands to loosen the shell. Start peeling from the wider end (where the air pocket is) for easier peeling. If the eggs are fresh, peeling can be more challenging, but the ice water bath helps.

Serve or Store:

You can serve the hard-boiled eggs immediately or store them in the refrigerator for later use. They make great snacks, salad toppings, or can be used in various dishes.

That's it! You now have perfect hard-boiled eggs ready for your favorite recipes or as a healthy snack.
