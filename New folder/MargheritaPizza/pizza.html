MARGHERITA PIZZA
RECENT POST

Ingredients :

For the Pizza Dough:

1 pound (about 3 1/4 cups) pizza dough, homemade or store-bought

Flour for dusting

For the Margherita Toppings:

1/2 cup tomato sauce or crushed San Marzano tomatoes

1 1/2 cups fresh mozzarella cheese, sliced or torn into pieces

1-2 cloves garlic, minced

Fresh basil leaves

Extra-virgin olive oil

Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

Preheat the Oven:

Preheat your oven to its highest temperature, typically around 500°F (260°C), or as high as it goes. If you have a pizza stone or pizza steel, place it in the oven while it preheats.

Prepare the Dough:

Dust your work surface with flour to prevent sticking. Roll out the pizza dough into a 12-inch (30 cm) circle. You can use a rolling pin or your hands to shape the dough. Transfer the rolled dough to a pizza peel or an inverted baking sheet dusted with flour or cornmeal to prevent sticking.

Assemble the Pizza:

Spread the tomato sauce evenly over the pizza dough, leaving a small border around the edge for the crust. Sprinkle the minced garlic over the sauce. Add the fresh mozzarella cheese evenly over the sauce.

Bake the Pizza:

Carefully slide the pizza onto the preheated pizza stone or baking sheet in the oven. Bake for 10-12 minutes, or until the crust is golden and the cheese is bubbling and slightly browned.

Finish with Fresh Basil and Olive Oil:

Remove the pizza from the oven and immediately top it with fresh basil leaves. Drizzle extra-virgin olive oil over the pizza, and season with a pinch of salt and freshly ground black pepper.

Slice and Serve:

Use a pizza cutter or a sharp knife to slice the Margherita pizza into wedges or squares. Serve hot and enjoy the simplicity and flavors of this classic Italian pizza.

Margherita pizza is best when it's fresh out of the oven, so serve it right away for the best taste and texture.
