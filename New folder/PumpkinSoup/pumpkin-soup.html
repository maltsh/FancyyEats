PUMPKIN SOUP
RECENT POST

Ingredients:

2 tablespoons butter or olive oil

1 medium onion, chopped

2 cloves garlic, minced

1 medium-sized pumpkin (about 3-4 pounds), peeled, seeded, and cut into chunks

4 cups vegetable or chicken broth

1/2 teaspoon ground cinnamon

1/4 teaspoon ground nutmeg

1/4 teaspoon ground ginger

Salt and pepper to taste

1 cup heavy cream (optional for a creamy soup)

Fresh parsley or chives for garnish (optional)

Instructions:

Prepare the Pumpkin:

Start by peeling the pumpkin and removing the seeds. Cut it into small, uniform chunks.

Sauté Onion and Garlic:

In a large soup pot, melt the butter (or heat the olive oil) over medium heat. Add the chopped onion and garlic and sauté until they become soft and translucent, but not browned, about 5 minutes.

Add Pumpkin and Spices:

Add the pumpkin chunks to the pot and stir to combine with the onions and garlic. Sprinkle in the ground cinnamon, nutmeg, and ginger. Cook for a few minutes to allow the spices to infuse the pumpkin.

Add Broth and Simmer:

Pour in the vegetable or chicken broth, making sure the pumpkin is mostly covered. Bring the mixture to a boil, then reduce the heat to a simmer. Cover the pot and let it cook for about 20-25 minutes or until the pumpkin is tender.

Blend the Soup:

Use an immersion blender or carefully transfer the soup in batches to a blender and puree until smooth. If using a blender, return the pureed soup to the pot.

Season and Add Cream:

Season the soup with salt and pepper to taste. If you prefer a creamy pumpkin soup, stir in the heavy cream at this stage. Heat the soup gently, but do not let it come to a boil.

Serve:

Ladle the pumpkin soup into bowls. Garnish with a drizzle of cream, fresh parsley or chives, and a pinch of ground nutmeg, if desired. Serve the soup hot and enjoy!

This pumpkin soup is perfect for a cozy fall or winter meal. You can also customize it

with additional spices or toppings to suit your taste, such as a sprinkle of toasted pumpkin seeds or croutons.
